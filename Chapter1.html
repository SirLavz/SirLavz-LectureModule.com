ðŸ“˜ Key Concepts

Data vs. Information: Data are raw facts; information is processed data with meaning. Metadata describes data characteristics and relationships.

Database: A shared, integrated structure storing related data and metadata.

DBMS: Software that manages data access, integrity, security, and concurrency.

Advantages of DBMS: Improved data sharing, security, integration, consistency, access, and decision-making.

Types of Databases: Single-user, multi-user, centralized, distributed, operational, analytical, structured, unstructured, XML-based.

Database System Environment: Includes hardware, software, people, procedures, and data.
