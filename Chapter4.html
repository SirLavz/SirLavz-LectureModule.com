ðŸ“˜ Key Concepts

Entity: A person, place, thing, or concept about which data is stored. Represented as a rectangle in ER diagrams.

Attribute: A property or characteristic of an entity. Can be simple, composite, single-valued, multivalued, or derived.

Relationship: An association between entities. Classified as 1:1, 1:M, or M:N.

Connectivity: Describes the type of relationship (e.g., one-to-many).

Cardinality: Specifies the minimum and maximum number of entity occurrences.

Participation: Can be optional or mandatory depending on business rules.

ðŸ”— Relationship Types

  • Unary: Relationship within a single entity (e.g., Employee supervises Employee)
  • Binary: Relationship between two entities (e.g., Student enrolls in Course)
  • Ternary: Relationship among three entities (e.g., Doctor prescribes Medication to Patient)
  • Recursive: Entity relates to itself (e.g., Part contains Part)

ðŸ§© Weak & Associative Entities

Weak Entity: Depends on another entity for existence. Its primary key is partially or fully derived from the parent entity.

Associative Entity: Also called a bridge entity. Used to implement M:N relationships and may contain additional attributes.
